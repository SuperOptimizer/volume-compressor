# Phase-1 entropy + quant bake-off — results

Generated by `harness/compare_c3d_c4d.py` on PHerc Paris 4 (AWS open data).
Inputs: **hires-256** (8 cached high-res 128³ center chunks assembled 2×2×2 into
a 256³ cube) and **coarse-256** (256³ crop of the coarse 384³ sub-region).
References built from their repos: c3d (linked static lib, 256³ atom,
`c3d_chunk_encode_at_q`) and c4d (CLI `encode`/`decode --q`). Both built clean.

Our codec: int-DCT-8³ + dead-zone + the new swappable **entropy** (rice | rlgr |
rans) × **quant HF-weighting** (flat | HF-protecting | content-adaptive),
independent chunks, fixed-q. Metrics: PSNR, MS-SSIM (per-axis-slice), GMSD
(3D-slice). q is the dead-zone step; c3d/c4d swept over their own q scales and
compared at equal PSNR (ratio-at-PSNR, not at equal q — the scales differ).

## Headlines

1. **The ratio emergency is largely fixed.** On hires at 38 dB the Phase-0
   rice-flat codec gets **6.5×**; the new **rans-hf gets 21.0× — a 3.2× jump** at
   identical quality, entirely from (a) coding the long HF zero-runs cheaply and
   (b) the frequency scan that makes those runs contiguous. On coarse the gain is
   smaller (4.8×→5.9×, ~1.2–1.5×) because that data has far less quantized-away
   HF mass for the zero-run coders to exploit.

2. **Plain Rice is the wrong coder at scroll ratios.** rice-flat/rice-hf are
   pinned near ~6× regardless of q on hires: Golomb-Rice cannot cheaply code the
   massive runs of zeros (≈1 bit/zero), so it never enters the high-ratio regime.
   This reproduces the red-team warning (PLAN §2 Entropy row).

3. **Entropy winner: static rANS, with RLGR a close, faster second.** rans-hf
   beats rlgr-hf at every operating point (21.0 vs 19.0 at 38 dB) because its
   per-chunk frequency table models the small-coefficient distribution better
   than RLGR's backward-adaptive Golomb parameter. RLGR costs nothing in table
   side-data and **decodes ~1.7× faster** (≈240 vs ≈145 MB/s, single-thread) and
   encodes faster too, so RLGR is the better *balanced* pick; rANS is the
   *max-ratio* pick.

4. **HF-protecting quant helps, content-adaptive (as built) hurts.** HF-weighting
   gives a consistent small lift (e.g. 20.7→21.0 at 38 dB) AND better MS-SSIM/GMSD
   at equal ratio — it protects edges/ink. The content-adaptive variant here is a
   *bounded, exactly-reversible dequant-reconstruction nudge* (no side bits, no bin
   reassignment); it slightly *lowers* ratio-at-PSNR because the nudge adds
   distortion at a fixed rate. A true content-adaptive per-block-QP would need
   side bits in the stream (a future lever); the safe nudge is not worth it.

5. **Gap to the references.** We are now within **~2× of c4d** and **~2.5× of c3d**
   at equal PSNR on hires (was ~6× for Phase-0 rice). The remaining gap is
   transform (c3d/c4d use CDF-9/7 wavelet, which compacts scroll energy better
   than the 8³ DCT at <1 bpp — the Phase-1 transform bake-off, in progress
   separately) and chunk-model (region-shared entropy tables). On coarse we are
   already within ~1.2× of c4d.

## Recommendation

Lead config for **max ratio**: `VC_ENTROPY=rans` + `VC_QWEIGHT=1` (HF-protecting).
Lead config for **balanced (speed)**: `VC_ENTROPY=rlgr` + `VC_QWEIGHT=1`.
Keep `rice`/`flat` only as the Phase-0 baseline. Drop the content-adaptive nudge
unless a side-bit per-block-QP form is built. The next ratio lever is the
transform (wavelet) — the entropy stage is no longer the bottleneck.

## Notes / caveats
- All 9 entropy×qweight configs round-trip exactly (`vc_test`, `vc_test_entropy`).
- rANS uses `unsigned __int128` reciprocal-multiply (no per-token divide), 12-bit
  freq table; the 512-byte/chunk table is amortized fine at 64³+ but dominates
  tiny inputs (see unit-test b/sym) — a region-shared table (PLAN chunk-model
  axis) would remove that.
- c3d's 256³ atom sees the 128-boundary seams of the assembled hires cube, which
  may modestly understate c3d here; its coarse/native numbers are clean.
- Reproduce: `python3 harness/compare_c3d_c4d.py --inputs both --configs all \
  --q 4 8 16 32 64 128`

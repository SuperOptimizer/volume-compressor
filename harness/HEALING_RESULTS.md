# Boundary-healing (deblocking) bake-off — results

Generated by `harness/bench_healing` on real **PHerc Paris 4** (AWS open data),
the same **hires-256** (8 hires 128³ center chunks assembled 2×2×2) and
**coarse-256** (256³ crop of the coarse 384³ sub-region) inputs the entropy/quant
bake-off used. Codec under test = the current winning stack: **integer
DCT-16³** (`VC_TRANSFORM=dct16`) + dead-zone subband quant, **HF-protecting**
(`VC_QWEIGHT=1`), **static rANS** (`VC_ENTROPY=rans`), independent 64³ chunks,
fixed-q. The healing filter is a **pure decode-side post-process** on the
reconstructed volume (no bitstream change, chunk independence + 16³ random
access preserved).

Metrics vs the ORIGINAL: PSNR, MS-SSIM (per-axis-slice), **GMSD** (the
blocking/smear metric — lower is better), **HaarPSI** (edge perceptual — higher
is better), **seam16** (mean |Δ| across all 16³ sub-block faces, all 3 axes),
**seamChk** (mean |Δ| across the 64³ chunk faces only), and **heal MB/s** (the
single-thread cost of the filter alone). Reproduce:
`build/bench_healing harness/refbuild/hires_256.u8 256 256 256 hires-256`.

Healing configs: `none` (passthrough), `deblock@{0.3,0.5,0.8,1.0}` (strength),
`cdef@{0.5,1.0}`.

## Headlines

1. **Deblocking decisively removes the seams the user reported — on hires.**
   At q=64 (36.7×, mid-target band) the 16³+chunk seam-step drops from
   **7.53 → 0.96 at deblock@0.3 (−87%)** and to **~0 at @1.0**, with PSNR
   essentially flat (36.01 → 35.87 at @0.3) and **MS-SSIM *improving*** (0.9410
   → 0.9447). At q=32 (21×), @0.3 cuts seam-step **6.04 → 3.36 (−44%)** at zero
   PSNR/SSIM cost. This is the smear+blocking fix: the filter is keyed on the
   quant step + local flatness, so it erases the flat DC steps a quantizer leaves
   at block/chunk faces while the per-sample delta clip (±tc, step-derived)
   protects real ink/fiber edges.

2. **GMSD is roughly flat under deblock, not worse.** At hires q=64, mild
   deblock@0.3 even nudges **GMSD down** (0.1155 → 0.1144). Stronger strengths
   trade a hair of GMSD/PSNR for near-total seam removal — the seam-step metric
   is where the visible win shows; GMSD (a global gradient-deviation statistic)
   is dominated by the bulk content, so it moves little. The mandate's "GMSD
   should drop" holds at mild strength and stays within noise at strong strength
   — crucially it does **not** rise, i.e. deblock does not introduce new
   structural distortion.

3. **No over-smoothing of real structure.** Across every operating point, mild
   deblock (@0.3) costs ≤0.17 dB PSNR and holds or *improves* MS-SSIM/HaarPSI.
   The unit test confirms the same property synthetically: on a smooth seam-free
   volume deblock@0.5 perturbs samples by mean 0.08/255 (≈identity), while it
   takes an injected ±10 DC step at every 16-face to ~0 and moves the volume
   closer to the clean source.

4. **CDEF is a different, complementary tool — not a seam filter.** On hires it
   *raises* PSNR (+0.8 dB at q=64), MS-SSIM, and HaarPSI and *lowers* GMSD
   (0.1155 → 0.1060) by cleaning directional ringing along fibers — a genuine
   quality lift — but it barely touches seam-step (7.53 → 4.75) because it does
   not target faces. On coarse-256 (noisier, low-frequency data with little
   directional ringing) CDEF **over-smooths and hurts** (PSNR 35.6 → 33.6 at
   q=32). So CDEF is only worth enabling on hi-res fiber-rich data, and even
   there as an *add-on* to deblock, never a replacement. It is also ~35× slower
   (~36 MB/s vs ~1200 MB/s) because of its per-8×8 direction search and the Y/X
   gather, and it does not autovectorize (the 8×8 directional kernel has
   bound/direction branches); deblock's normal-direction stencil **does**
   vectorize (`-fopt-info-vec` confirms `deblock.c:68` → 16-byte vectors).

5. **Speed cost of deblock is negligible.** ~1.1–1.3 GB/s single-threaded for
   the whole-volume 3-axis pass — i.e. the filter adds well under a millisecond
   per 256³ and is a rounding error next to the ~145–240 MB/s entropy decode.

6. **Coarse benefits less and needs gentler strength.** Coarse seams are larger
   in absolute terms (it is downscaled, so each chunk face spans more real
   structure) but the data is also noisier, so strong deblock costs more PSNR
   there (−1.1 dB at @1.0, q=64). Mild deblock@0.3 still cuts seam-step ~30–45%
   at ≤0.1 dB. The same default (mild) is safe for both.

## Decision & recommended default

**Deblocking reduces the 16³+chunk seam-step by 44–90% in the 20–50× target band
with ≤0.2 dB PSNR cost and no MS-SSIM/HaarPSI/GMSD regression at mild strength,
at ~1.2 GB/s.** It directly addresses the SMEAR + BLOCKING the user reported on
real (esp. downscaled) scroll chunks: the visible artifact is the flat per-face
DC step, and that is exactly what the seam-step metric measures and what deblock
removes while the QP-derived delta clip keeps real edges intact.

**Recommended default healing config: `VC_HEALING = vc_heal_deblock` at strength
≈0.3–0.5** (mild). 0.3 is the conservative pick (seam −44–87%, PSNR/SSIM flat);
0.5 if the seams remain visible (seam −70–98%, ≤0.5 dB PSNR). Avoid @1.0 except
for deliberately seam-free viewing — it zeroes seams but costs ~0.5–1.1 dB.

**CDEF**: ship as an *optional* extra pass for hi-res fiber-rich volumes where
ringing is the complaint (it lifts PSNR/GMSD/HaarPSI there); **leave it off by
default** — it over-smooths coarse/noisy data and is ~35× slower. Do not use it
as the seam filter; pair it after deblock if both ringing and seams need fixing.

## Notes / caveats / mandate compliance

- Pure C23, no hand intrinsics. Deblock's inner stencil autovectorizes
  (`-fopt-info-vec`: `src/healing/deblock.c:68` → 16-byte vectors); CDEF's
  directional kernel does not (acceptable — it is the optional, off-by-default
  filter). Single-threaded reentrant, heap scratch (CDEF's gather plane), in-place
  on the decoded u8 volume.
- The filter runs as a post-process on the decoded volume here, so the bench
  needed no codec internals; the same call is wired as the `VC_HEALING` block for
  in-codec use (`config.h`).
- Seam metric covers all 16-grid faces (sub-block + chunk are a superset/subset);
  `seamChk` isolates the 64³ chunk faces and tracks `seam16` closely, confirming
  the filter heals chunk faces and sub-block faces alike.
- `ctest` green (4/4 incl. the new `healing` test: identity-on-smooth +
  seam-reduction-on-blocky).
- Operating points: hires reaches the 20–50× target band at q=32–64; coarse
  compresses less (5–16× here) so its absolute ratios are lower, but the seam
  trend is identical.
